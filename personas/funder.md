# Persona — Funder

A program officer or grants manager at a foundation, multilateral, or government agency considering whether to fund the program design (or fund Dimagi to deliver it). Reads the demo to assess accountability, evidence quality, scale credibility, and value-for-money.

## Priorities

- **Accountability surface.** Can they see what was promised, what was delivered, and where the gaps are — without having to ask?
- **Evidence quality.** Is data verified (Layer A/B/C), spot-checked, or self-reported? How does the program handle photo-quality / FLW-quality issues?
- **Cost-efficiency narrative.** $-per-visit, $-per-beneficiary, $-per-verified-outcome — clean numbers they can put in a board memo.
- **Risk + mitigations.** What goes wrong? What's the program's response? How does it degrade gracefully?
- **Scalability story.** Is this 50 vendors as a pilot, or 5,000 vendors as a program? What changes between?

## Language

- "Beneficiary" / "participant" / "respondent" — outcome-side, not delivery-side.
- "Per-FLW productivity" / "data-quality rate" / "verified completion" — they want metrics with denominators.
- "Cycle" or "wave" — they think in fundable units (a 6-month cycle, a 3-year program).
- "Layer A / B / C" verification (when present) reads as professional accountability, not jargon.

## Dashboards of interest

- **Headline metric panel** — total visits, total verified, $-per-verified — the slide that goes in the funder report.
- **Quality verification flow** — Layer A automated → Layer B AI → Layer C manual; show that the program has all three.
- **Geographic coverage** — does the program reach the populations it claims to reach? Maps with density not just dots.
- **Trend lines** — week-over-week or month-over-month, not just snapshots.
- **Anomaly handling** — when something goes wrong (a struggling FLW, a fraud flag, a missed area), what does the program do? Show the handle, not just the issue.

## Turn-offs

- Soft language without numbers ("improved outcomes" with no denominator).
- Vendor / FLW PII in the demo — they want anonymized, not raw.
- "We're working on it" answers — they're funding what works, not what might.
- Single-cohort / single-region demos labeled as pilots when funder is asked to fund full scale.
- Polished UI with empty data behind it — they read past the chrome.

## What lands

- Headline numbers visible in the first 30 seconds: "850 vendors, $2.30/visit, 92% data quality, 18% flagged for review."
- A worked example of one anomaly going through the full handle: detected → assigned → coached → re-measured → improved.
- Cost numbers tied to the budget line they're funding ("at this rate, the $1.5M ask delivers 500K verified visits over 18 months").
- Clear failure modes + mitigations: "FLW dropout rate is 8% historically; we cover with a 15% over-recruitment buffer."
- Comparison to a baseline ("compared to standard market surveys, this is 4× faster and 30% cheaper per verified data point").
